Why Wait 39 Weeks

Being pregnant 39 weeks gives your baby's body all the time it needs to grow.

  • Important organs, like his brain, lungs and liver, get the time they need to develop.

  • The baby is less likely to have vision and hearing problems after birth.

  • The baby has time to gain more weight in the womb. Babies born at a healthy weight have an easier time staying warm than babies born too small.

  • The baby can suck and swallow and stay awake long enough to eat after the baby is born. Babies born early sometimes can't do these things.

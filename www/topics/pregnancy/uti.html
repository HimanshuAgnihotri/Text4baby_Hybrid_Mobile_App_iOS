Urinary Tract Infection

Urinary Tract Infections (UTI) are bacterial infections along your urinary tract.

A simple bladder infection often has no symptoms at all, and you'll only find out you have one when your practitioner runs a routine urine test at your monthly visit. Other UTIs symptoms can include:

  • Pain or burning when urinating

  • More frequent, and more urgent, urination

  • Cramps and pain in the lower part of your abdomen

  • Cloudy urine, or urine that has a strong or nasty smell

Treating a UTI is done by taking a full course of prescribed antibiotics specific to the type of bacteria found in your urine sample.

Here are steps to reduce your chances of developing a UTI during your pregnancy:

  • Drink plenty of water

  • Check out your urine color often - if it's dark, not straw-colored, you're not getting enough fluids and could be setting yourself up for a UTI

  • Don't hold it!

  • Empty your bladder when you urinate (lean forward when peeing)

  • Wipe from front to rear after you use the toilet

  • Clean your genitals and the areas surrounding them, particularly after having sex

  • Avoid using genital sprays, powders, and especially douches

  • Wear all-cotton undergarments

  • Take showers rather than baths whenever possible. Avoid bubble baths (which tend to irritate your urethral opening) and make sure that the tub is really clean (and rinsed thoroughly) before using it

Call your practitioner immediately if you have any symptoms of a UTI.

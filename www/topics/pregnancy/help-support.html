Help & Support

Domestic Violence

National Domestic Violence Hotline

Provides crisis intervention, information and referral to victims of domestic violence, perpetrators, friends and families. The Hotline has access to more than 4,000 shelters and domestic violence programs across the United States, Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands. The Hotline is toll-free, confidential and anonymous and operates 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.

800-799-7233 www.thehotline.org

Finding Child Care

Child Care Aware

Helps families learn more about the elements of quality child care and how to locate programs in their communities.

800-424-2246 www.ChildCareAware.org

National Hunger

WHYHunger's National Hunger Hotline

Connects individuals in need to emergency food in their community, government assistance programs, and various social services.

866-348-6479 www.WhyHunger.org/FindFood

Postpartum Depression

Postpartum Support International (PSI)

Dedicated to helping women suffering from perinatal mood and anxiety disorders, including postpartum depression. Family, friends and healthcare providers can also call so moms and moms-to-be can get the support they need and recover (toll-free in English and Spanish).

800-994-4773 www.Postpartum.net

Quitting Smoking

Smokefree.gov

Connects you to counseling and information about quitting smoking. Help is also available via live chat online.

800-784-8669 www.Smokefree.gov

Substance Abuse Help

U.S. Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration's National Helpline

A confidential, free, 24-hour-a-day, 365-day-a-year, information service, in English and Spanish, for individuals and family members facing substance abuse issues.

800-662-4357 www.samhsa.gov/treatment

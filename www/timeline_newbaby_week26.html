Mom this Week

Are you still eating well, mom? Make sure to eat breakfast every day. You may not have much time, but your body needs the energy (especially when you're lacking sleep!). Eggs, yogurt, fruit, and oatmeal are healthy options to keep you full.

Baby's Development

  • Baby at 6 Months

By 6 months your baby has almost doubled in weight since birth. At this point, your baby's growth will start to slow down. You can start to feed your baby solid foods. Introduce one food at a time, waiting a few days between new foods to find out if baby has any allergic reactions. Give baby soft and small bites. Continue to breastfeed and bottle feed, as necessary.

Does it feel impossible to keep up with the cleaning and laundry? Try to let it go. Clear up some of the clutter, but try not to worry about keeping a spotless space. All baby needs is a safe place to play.

Baby's Development

  • Baby at 9 Months

Now that baby is starting to eat solid foods, be careful to avoid any foods that could cause chocking such as peanuts, popcorn, hot dogs, carrots, celery sticks, whole grapes, raisins, whole beans, hard candy, tough meat, or large pieces of food. Gradually increase food and provide a variety of foods and textures. Continue breastfeeding if both you and baby still want to. Real fruit (mashed or pureed) are healthier for baby than juice.
